Biryani

Biryani is a richly aromatic and flavorful South Asian rice dish made by layering fragrant basmati rice with marinated meat—commonly chicken, beef, mutton, or fish—and a blend of spices such as cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and cumin. The dish often includes yogurt, fried onions, fresh herbs like cilantro and mint, and sometimes boiled eggs or potatoes, depending on the regional style. It is typically slow-cooked through a method called dum, where the pot is sealed and gently steamed, allowing the flavors to meld deeply into every grain of rice.

Originating from the Indian subcontinent, biryani has many regional variations—Hyderabadi, Lucknowi, Sindhi, and Karachi-style, to name a few—each with its own unique spices and cooking techniques. Though often associated with celebrations and feasts, biryani holds a special place in everyday cuisine as well. It’s more than just food; it’s a cultural experience that reflects centuries of history, trade, and culinary fusion across the Muslim world and South Asia.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken or beef (cut into pieces)
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Biryani masala
  • Fresh coriander and mint
  • Saffron or food color (optional)
  • Oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Wash and soak the rice for 30 minutes, then boil until 70% cooked.
  2. In a pot, heat oil and fry the onions until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and fry for a minute.
  4. Add meat, yogurt, tomatoes, biryani masala, and salt. Cook until meat is tender.
  5. Layer cooked rice over the meat mixture. Top with coriander, mint, and saffron.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat (dum) for 15-20 minutes.
  7. Mix gently before serving. Enjoy!
